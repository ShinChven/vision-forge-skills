# Pose & Composition Library

## Camera Angles
- **Eye Level**: Standard view, neutral perspective.
- **Low Angle**: Camera looks up, making the subject appear powerful or heroic.
- **High Angle**: Camera looks down, making the subject appear smaller or more vulnerable.
- **Bird's Eye / Top Down**: Directly above the subject.
- **Worm's Eye**: Extremely low angle, looking straight up.
- **Dutch Angle / Canted**: Tilted horizon, creates tension or disorientation.

## Framing & Shot Types
- **Full Body Shot**: Entire character from head to toe.
- **Cowboy Shot**: Mid-thigh up (classic western framing).
- **Medium Shot**: Waist up.
- **Medium Close-up**: Chest up.
- **Close-up**: Focuses on the head and shoulders.
- **Extreme Close-up**: Focuses on a specific detail (e.g., eyes, hand).
- **Profile**: Side view of the character.
- **3/4 View**: Character is turned slightly away from the camera.

## Pose Categories
- **Action/Dynamic**: Running, jumping, mid-air, fighting, lunging, sword-swing.
- **Casual/Standing**: Leaning against a wall, crossing arms, hand in pocket, contrapposto.
- **Sitting/Reclining**: Sitting on a throne, lounging on a sofa, sitting cross-legged.
- **Emotional/Expressive**: Cowering, reaching out, triumphant, contemplative.
- **Martial Arts/Combat**: Guard stance, high kick, meditation pose, drawing a weapon.

## Terminology
- **Contrapposto**: An asymmetrical arrangement of the human figure in which the line of the arms and shoulders contrasts with, while balancing, those of the hips and legs.
- **Foreshortening**: The technique of depicting an object or human body in a picture so as to produce an illusion of projection or extension in space.
- **S-Curve**: A pose that creates a fluid 'S' shape with the body's alignment.
